Sorting Questions

Why is quicksort generally better than mergesort?

Typically, quicksort is significantly faster in practice than other O(nlogn) algorithms, because its inner loop can be efficiently implemented on most architectures, and in most real-world data, it is possible to make design choices which minimize the probability of requiring quadratic time.

Under what circumstance will Bubble sort perform poorly?

If the array is completely sorted except the least element is last, then the algorithm will take O(n2) bubbling it down. Selection sort will always take O(n2) operations.

Real world application?

In the real-world, it is rare that the data is completely random. Based on the data set, it is usually best to use a combination of sorting algorithms.
